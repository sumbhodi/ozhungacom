DUALITY  ·  THE SPLIT
In Here / Out There — and why both halves are suspect
The Oldest Split in Philosophy
Substance Dualism
Descartes (17th c.) made it formal: mind is res cogitans — thinking stuff. Body is res extensa — extended stuff. Two fundamentally different substances.
The Problem
There is no immaterial "stuff" the way atoms and quarks are material stuff. Dualism is a category error — it names the experience of a split but doesn't explain it.
The Useful Error
Waste-bin categories can still lead somewhere real. Dualism names a genuine felt experience — the sense that there is an in here doing the experiencing, and an out there being experienced.
Two Ways to Resolve It
Out There is the illusion
Simulation theory. Idealism. The world is mind-made. We're in a rendering engine.
In Here is the illusion ←
The "observer" — the sense of a separate subject inside a skull — is the construction. Vedanta and neuroscience agree here: the self is the model, not the modeler.
From the primal split, we make all the others
Good / Bad  ·  Hot / Cold  ·  Light / Dark  ·  Sacred / Profane. We are dipole machines — take any spectrum, collapse it to two poles.
In other words: we are all on the spectrum, and a little bipolar.
That is not a bug. That is how a brain navigates a continuous universe it has divided into parts. The question is whether we mistake the map for the territory — or the witness for the witnessed.
