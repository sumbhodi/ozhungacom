ब्रह्म
BRAHMAN
The Undivided Ground of All Being
All things arise from, subsist in, and return to this — not a place, not a God, but the fabric itself.
In Advaita Vedanta (non-dual philosophy), Brahman is the single, undivided reality. No second thing exists. The universe is not a container that holds things — it is what things are. There are no seams, no inside and outside, no pockets of here and there. The whole show is one continuous movement.
The koshas, the Atman — these are Brahman wearing increasingly transparent veils. Peel them all back and you don't find a self meeting a world. You find the universe being aware of itself through this particular arrangement of matter. The individual witness and the witnessed are the same thing looking at itself.
This is why the universe is always losing its keys. No pockets.
Aham Brahmasmi  ·  I am Brahman
— Brihadaranyaka Upanishad
